01

Business Challenges

As the organization scaled across branches, operations remained dependent on fragmented, manual workflows.
Without system-level visibility, failures accumulated silently — undetected, unassigned, and unresolved until they surfaced as larger problems.

Content Governance Risk

Materials With No Oversight

Teaching materials were submitted through untracked channels — email threads,
chat messages, verbal handoffs — leaving no structured record of what was submitted
or whether it met curriculum requirements.

Impact: Curriculum alignment could not be verified — delivery commitments to students had no audit trail to detect or correct misalignment

Service Delivery Risk

Fragmented Request Management

Printing requests reached teaching assistants through inconsistent channels, with no defined intake process, no confirmation of receipt, and no escalation path for overdue items. Ownership was assumed rather than assigned.

Impact: Classroom readiness could not be guaranteed — directly undermining service quality and student satisfaction

Financial Integrity Risk

Invoices Without Verification

Month-end invoices arrived without sufficient supporting documentation — leaving Finance with no basis to reconcile amounts against actual operational requests before payment was processed.

Impact: Without a verifiable request record, billing discrepancies had no detection mechanism — creating cost overruns and financial leakage

Planning Capability Risk

Data-Blind Budget Planning

Class sizes and material demand fluctuated throughout the year, yet no system existed to capture demand patterns across branches. Budget decisions were made without reliable data, resulting in planning driven by intuition rather than evidence.

Impact: Cost forecasting and vendor negotiations remained reactive — limiting cost efficiency and long-term planning capability

Solution Approach

No new tools. No additional budget. No steep learning curve.
The solution was designed around constraints — and Google Workspace, already embedded in daily operations, was the right foundation to build on.

Workflow architecture
Technology Stack

• Forms — User interface with validation
• Sheets — Data storage and analytics
• Gmail — Notification automation
• Drive — Centralized file repository

Why this Stack?

• Zero additional cost — CapEx & Opex
• Familiar UI/UX — No adoption barrier
• Capable for the required business logic — no external servers needed

04

The Workflow

Built on existing infrastructure, the workflow connects every stage of the materials process into a single traceable system,
from the moment a request is submitted to the point Finance reconciles the invoice.

01 / Entry Layer
User Enablement

A self-service tutorial embedded directly in the form interface — giving teachers the context needed to submit requests correctly from the first attempt, without requiring live training or ongoing support from the system owner.

02 / Input Layer
Request Form

A structured Google Form with built-in validation enforces data quality at the point of entry — ensuring every request is complete and correctly formatted before it reaches the processing layer.

03 / Processing Layer
Automation Engine

Form submission triggers a parallel automation sequence — logging the request to the Master Sheet, notifying the responsible teaching assistant, and confirming receipt to the requester. No manual coordination required at any step.

04 / Data Layer
Master Sheet

The Master Sheet serves as the single source of truth — logging every request with timestamp, requester, assigned TA, status, and materials list. Request history is queryable, auditable, and accessible to both operations and finance.

05 / Archive Layer
Content Repository

Teaching materials are automatically archived to Drive upon submission — establishing the content audit trail the organization had never had. Folder-level permissions maintain accessibility for relevant staff while preserving control over sensitive files.

06 / Analytic Layer
Reporting View

Aggregates request data into a structured reporting view — providing Finance with a reconciliation baseline against vendor invoices and the demand visibility needed for evidence-based budget planning.

05

Business Value Delivered

The system replaced four informal workarounds with auditable, process-level controls — each outcome mapped directly to a structural gap that had no resolution path before.

✔ Content Accountability

Commitments Tracked. Gaps Closed.

• Content Oversight – Materials are logged and traceable, making curriculum alignment auditable on demand rather than assumed.
• Delivery Accountability – Every submission is timestamped and assigned, creating a clear record of who committed to what — and when.

✔ Operational Reliability

Reliability Built Into the Process

• Intake Control – A centralized intake channel replaced fragmented request paths, eliminating missed or delayed fulfillment.
• Resource Optimization – Standardized workflows reduced reactive coordination load, freeing TAs for higher-value student support.

✔ Financial Control

From Blind Payment to Verified Spend

• Cost Visibility – Request records provided a reconciliation baseline, enabling Finance to verify invoices before payment rather than after.
• Leakage Prevention – Documented evidence closed the gap for unchecked billing — blind payments became structurally impossible.

✔ Planning Intelligence

Data Where There Was None

• Consumption Tracking – Visible demand patterns provide a baseline for budget planning and vendor negotiations.
• Data Maturity – The organization moved from zero operational data to a structured, analytics-ready foundation.
